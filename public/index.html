Gift Rules Guidance

Key Rules Summary

Prohibited Sources (Rule 8)

  • Those seeking official action from your agency
  • Those doing business with your agency
  • Those regulated by your agency
  • Those whose interests may be affected by your duties

Exceptions (Rule 11)

  • Gifts from immediate relatives
  • Gifts based on personal relationships
  • Opportunities available to the general public
  • Nominal value gifts on special occasions

Key Definitions

  • Nominal Value: ≤10 days' national minimum wage
  • Immediate Relative: Spouse, parents, siblings, lineal ascendants/descendants
  • Fair Market Value: Retail cost of similar item
  • Gift: Anything of economic value with no consideration in return

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I accept gifts from family members?

Yes, gifts from immediate relatives are allowed if clearly motivated by the relationship rather than your official position (Rule 11(b)).

What is considered "nominal value"?

Nominal value is an amount not more than ten days' national minimum wage (Rule 47(h)). You must check the current minimum wage.

What happens if I miss the 24-hour declaration deadline?

You may be subject to penalties under Rule 36-37. It's important to declare as soon as possible even if late.
